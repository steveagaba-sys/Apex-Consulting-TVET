Context & Mandate

The enactment of the TVET Act 2025 is a landmark commitment. However, as a public sector start-up, the Council faces the "structural inertia" of merging five disparate entities. Without a defined Target Operating Model (TOM) pursuant to Section 17(2)(d), the Council risks operational bottlenecks that will delay the registration of critical tradespeople (mechanics, health workers, ICT specialists).

Gap Analysis

Current Operating Model Risks

The transition period creates high ambiguity. We have identified three critical friction points that the TOM must resolve immediately to prevent functional failure.

Institutional Fragmentation

Legacy of 5+ repealed acts

The Issue: Merging distinct organizational cultures, asset registers, and IT systems. The previous "Joint Inspection Team" model is too slow for mass licensing.

Impact: Delays in licensing mechanics, craftsmen, and health workers.

Jurisdictional Conflict

Internal & External Overlaps

Internal: Secretariat vs. Assessment Boards (UVTAB/UHPAB). Executive overreach risks technical credibility.

External: S. 30(3) vs S. 30(5) - TVET Council licensing vs. NCHE accreditation for university diplomas.

Fiscal Vulnerability

SDF Setup & Levy Management

The Issue: Managing the new 1% SD Levy. Needs a transparent Special Revenue Fund (SRF) structure to comply with PFM standards.

Requirement: Robust governance to prevent underspending or misuse.

The Apex Solution

Integrated Target Operating Model

Three Strategic Action Frameworks designed to directly address the legislative mandates of the TVET Act 2025 and enable immediate operational readiness.

Action I: Streamlined Digital Registration

Urgent Priority: Consolidating 5+ fragmented processes into one Digital Registry System (DRS). This framework targets the removal of the slow "Joint Inspection" model to meet the Dec 15 deadline.

Goal: Reduce "Time-to-License" by 60% via Automated Validation.

Step 1: Digital Submission

Applicant (Institution, Trainer, or Artisan) submits unified application + digital payment via DRS Portal. This replaces manual paper trails.

  • Unified Application Form
  • Integrated Digital Payment

Step 2: Automated Validation

The System performs instantaneous checks for completeness, document validity, and trainer CPD (Continuous Professional Development) compliance.

Incomplete apps are rejected instantly with specific feedback, reducing backlog.

Step 3: Risk-Based Review

Centralized Licensing Unit applies a risk matrix.

Low Risk (Renewals):
Waive physical inspection.
High Risk (New):
Trigger mandatory physical audit.

Step 4: Audit & Assessment

If required, Delegated Independent Auditors conduct site visits. Technical competency is verified via interface with Assessment Boards (UVTAB/UHPAB).

Findings submitted electronically via tablet apps.

Step 5: Final Publish

Executive Director (or delegate) grants final approval. Digital License issued to applicant wallet. Public Registry updated immediately.
